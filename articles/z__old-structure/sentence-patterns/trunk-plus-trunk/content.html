A trunk is a complete sentence, because it has SVO. Notice the period at the end! One way to add more information to the same sentence is by adding another trunk. The result is a longer complete sentence made of SVO + SVO, which we call Trunk + Trunk or T, + T. Note: If you have trouble with run-on sentences, your solution starts here. In SVO + SVO, you must fill the + position with one of these joiners (connector words), NOT a comma: and but so or ; (pronounced semi-colon; used instead of and) for (meaning because) yet (meaning but) NOT just a comma! (comma before the joiner) Examples: These two trees are different, but they grew so close they almost look connected. The one on the left is probably a white birch; you can tell by its color and the peeling bark.
