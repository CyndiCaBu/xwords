This is my friend's old blender; she bought it in the early 1960s. That means she has had it for around 50 years, although she hasn't used it much lately. In fact, she recently decided that it would be better to give it to her daughter, who would use it much more.

When she first got this blender, my friend made drinks and baby food in it. She used to make milkshakes and ice cream floats, especially with vanilla ice cream and raspberry soda. She told me that people didn't have smoothies in those days, but when it was really hot outside she would crush ice in the blender for cold drinks like iced tea and lemonade. She also pureed fruit in the blender for her kids when they were very young.

Although soup might sound like a strange dish for hot weather, my friend said she used to make at least 2 kinds of soup in the blender, too. One was a deeply colored version of borscht, which is made with beets, cabbage and other vegetables. Another favorite was a cold potato and leek soup that is known by its French name, vichyssoise. My friend explained that when she made that, she would cook the potatoes and leeks in chicken stock and then let it cool. When it wasnâ€™t too hot, she pureed it in the blender and then transferred it to a bowl to add cream until it reached the right consistency. She said that nowadays she would probably use a hand-held immersion blender to puree soup right in the pot, but back in the 1960s she didn't know anyone with that kind of blender. She wasn't even sure if you could buy one at that time.

Years later, after my friend had lived in Ecuador for a couple years, she used the blender to mix frozen fruit drinks. One of her favorites is mora, a type of blackberry. You can see juice made from it in the image here.
